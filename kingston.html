Overview

The Problem

The City of Kingston was managing multiple grant programs using a fragmented set of tools — email, Excel, Dropbox, and Google Drive. This created systemic inefficiencies for staff and applicant organisations alike.

Manual & Fragmented

Applications arrived via email and were tracked across disconnected spreadsheets, Dropbox folders, and Google Drive docs with no central system.

Screening Bottleneck

Manual screening consumed up to one full week per grant cycle. Incomplete applications caused by missing signatures were the primary cause of delays.

No Cross-Department Visibility

Different departments couldn't see each other's applicants. Duplicate applications were discovered by accident, well after time had been spent on review.

Partner Organisations Left Out

Organisations like Kingston Arts Council managed core administrative functions entirely outside City systems, creating data silos and version control issues.

Methodology

Three-Phase Research

A rigorous multi-phase approach was used to uncover pain points from multiple perspectives — external benchmarking, internal staff experience, and partner organisations.

1

Competitive Benchmarking

Six-Municipality Landscape Analysis

Conducted secondary research across Thunder Bay, Kamloops, Belleville, Vancouver, Ottawa, and Halifax to benchmark grant management approaches, identify best-in-class patterns, and surface gaps in Kingston's current model.

2

Primary Research · March 6, 2026

Staff Focus Group (5 Participants)

Facilitated a structured focus group with five City of Kingston staff members across three departments. Surfaced process pain points, workflow inefficiencies, and unmet needs from the people doing the work every day.

3

Stakeholder Interview

Partner Perspective: Kingston Arts Council

Conducted an in-depth interview with Nicole Daniels at Kingston Arts Council to understand the partner organisation experience — how they submit, track, and report on grants from outside the City's internal systems.

Research Insights

Key Findings

01

Manual application screening consumes up to one full week per grant cycle.

02

Missing signatures are the single most common cause of incomplete applications.

03

The jury review process relies entirely on Dropbox and paper forms — no digital audit trail.

04

Zero cross-departmental visibility means duplicate applicants are found by accident.

05

Post-award workload is substantial and chronically underestimated by current planning processes.

06

Partner organisations manage all administrative functions outside City systems entirely.

Design Solution

Centralised Portal Design

Proposed a unified grants management portal with automated workflows, integrated jury tools, and multi-user access for both City staff and partner organisations.

Auto Completeness Validation

Real-time form validation prevents incomplete submissions before they reach staff review queues.

Adaptive Application Forms

Dynamic forms that adjust sections and required fields based on grant program type automatically.

Integrated Jury Management

Digital scoring with time-limited access for jury members. Automated confidentiality with no manual enforcement needed.

Multi-User Access

Role-based permissions for City staff and partner organisations like Kingston Arts Council.

Duplicate Detection

Cross-department applicant lookup with automated alerts when the same organisation applies across multiple programs.

Post-Award Automation

Automated agreement generation, payment scheduling, and progress reporting with built-in compliance checklists.

Impact

Expected Outcomes

Reduce screening time from one week to one business day.

Reduce incomplete applications by 70 percent through real-time validation.

Automate jury confidentiality through time-limited portal access.

Give partner organisations operational control through multi-user permissions.
