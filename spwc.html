Overview

Developed a comprehensive social media marketing plan for Sandy Pines Wildlife Centre, a non-profit wildlife rehabilitation organisation in Kingston, Ontario. The existing presence suffered from inconsistent posting, outdated Facebook visuals, and weak fundraising calls to action.

The strategy addressed all three platforms with a unified campaign framework built around two primary audience personas — Carol Thompson (47, emotionally driven Facebook donor) and Rick Saunders (38, action-oriented secondary audience) — and a peak-season campaign designed to capitalise on spring rescue season and Mother's Day giving behaviour.

$0 Fundraising Goal

Challenges Identified

What We
Needed to Fix

01

No posting consistency — irregular Instagram and TikTok schedules meant the audience lost trust in the brand's reliability and content value.

02

Outdated Facebook visuals — the brand's flagship community platform had stale imagery and no cohesive visual identity across posts.

03

Weak fundraising CTAs — donation and volunteer recruitment posts lacked urgency, emotional pull, or specific calls to action that prompted action.

04

No urgency-driven content strategy — there was no framework connecting seasonal events or rescue peaks to content calendars or donor campaigns.

Campaign Strategy

The 5 Content
Pillars

Pillar 01

Rescue Stories

First-person rescue narratives across all platforms. Short-form video on TikTok/Reels, long-form photo essays on Facebook — built to emotionally activate the Carol persona.

Pillar 02

Educational Content

Wildlife facts, seasonal awareness posts, and care guides. Positions SPWC as a trusted local authority and earns algorithmic reach through saves and shares.

Pillar 03

Mother's Day Fundraiser

Targeted donation campaign leveraging Mother's Day as an emotional giving moment — timed to peak spring rescue season for maximum donor relevance.

Pillar 04

Urgency Posts

Crisis moments, high-need rescues, and volunteer shortfalls. Real-time posts activating Rick's action-oriented profile and driving immediate community response.

Pillar 05

Behind the Scenes

Day-in-the-life content showing staff and volunteers in action. Builds community authenticity, reduces donor scepticism, and nurtures repeat giving.
