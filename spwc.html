Social Media Marketing Plan

Sandy Pines Wildlife Centre, Social Media Marketing Plan

Role
Social Media Strategist, Content Planner
Team
Joel Varghese, Srishti Tiwari, Vienna Fernandes

Overview

Developed a comprehensive social media marketing plan for Sandy Pines Wildlife Centre, a non-profit wildlife rehabilitation organisation in Kingston Ontario. The plan addressed inconsistent posting, weak fundraising calls to action, and underutilised platforms, and proposed a full campaign strategy across Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok.

Key Challenges

  • Inconsistent Instagram and TikTok posting schedule.
  • Outdated Facebook visuals.
  • Weak calls to action for fundraising and volunteer recruitment.
  • No urgency-driven content strategy.

Strategy and Approach

Conducted a full social media audit across Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok. Identified two primary personas: Carol Thompson, a local emotionally engaged donor aged 47 active on Facebook, and Rick Saunders, a hands-on secondary audience member aged 38 who responds to action-based content.

Proposed a campaign called Spring Into Action targeting the peak spring rescue season and Mother's Day as an emotional giving moment. Defined five content pillars:

  • Rescue Stories
  • Educational Content
  • Mother's Day Fundraiser
  • Urgency Posts
  • Behind the Scenes

Built a platform-specific strategy with Facebook as the anchor platform, Instagram for fundraising and awareness, and TikTok for brand expansion and volunteer awareness. Created a full content calendar, caption and hashtag guide, and a $500 per month budget breakdown.

Results Targets

  • Raise $10,000 by May 31.
  • Recruit 10 new volunteers by July 31.
  • Increase Instagram engagement by 25 percent.
  • Grow Facebook followers by 15 percent from 24,000 to 27,600.
  • Grow Instagram followers by 20 percent from 3,800 to 4,560.
